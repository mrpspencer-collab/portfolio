Following the NHL.tv app redesign for connected devices, user research revealed a consistent pattern: fans used the app primarily for live games while consuming highlights and team content elsewhere. I led the design of a team-centric experience — shifting the app from league-first to fan-first.

## The Challenge

<div class="cs-split">
  <div class="cs-split-body">
    <p>The existing app was organized around the league. But behavioral data told a different story: most views originated from individual team fans — people who cared deeply about one team and used NHL.tv as a way to follow them across the season.</p>
    <p>The core challenge: redesign the experience around fans' favorite teams without alienating users who preferred the existing league-centric navigation.</p>
  </div>

## Content Strategy

<div class="cs-split">
  <div class="cs-split-body">
    <p>The team conducted ideation sessions mapping available content feeds and data against television design best practices. We considered the full seasonal arc — not just live game states, but pre-game anticipation, post-game recap, and the off-season experience when no games were scheduled.</p>

## App Map & Information Architecture

<div class="cs-split">
  <div class="cs-split-body">
    <p>The team home addition changed the first-launch flow. Users could designate either Scores or Team as their default landing page. Accessing Team home required setting a "Favorite" team — inheritable from NHL accounts.</p>
    <p>Complexity came from earlier product decisions enabling multiple "Favorites" and "Follow" categories. User data showed fans understood these distinctions and wanted the flexibility — so we preserved it rather than simplifying it away.</p>

## In-line Player

The in-line player emerged from sprint work — a live-look feature providing broadcast sensibility directly in the team home context.

<div class="cs-split">
  <div class="cs-split-body">
    <p>When a favorite team is playing, the live game loads automatically into the featured tile. Users can immediately launch fullscreen or stay in the team home context. Audio mutes when users disengage while video continues playing — a familiar pattern from broadcast apps, applied with purpose.</p>

## Impact

Team home launched for the new season to positive stakeholder and fan feedback via social channels. Customization options preserved the Scores screen preference — preventing the typical backlash that follows a redesign. NHL.tv achieved 33% year-over-year subscriber growth, with marketing directly leveraging the differentiated broadcast-alternative experience.

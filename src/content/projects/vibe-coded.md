Claude Code and Figma Make have changed how I prototype. What used to take a sprint now takes an afternoon — sometimes 30 minutes. The experiments below range from functioning tools in active use to interactive prototypes that made abstract concepts tangible enough to ship or kill.

      <h3 class="cs-exp-title">KPI Status Layer</h3>
      <p class="cs-exp-desc">A neutral-palette analytics dashboard needed positive/negative signaling that survived all four common types of color vision deficiency. 14 treatment concepts — scrims, edge bars, hatching, glow shadows, shape coding — each viewable through live color blindness simulation with a per-concept A11y audit.</p>
      <p class="cs-exp-takeaway">Color is decoration. Shape and motion are signal.</p>

      <h3 class="cs-exp-title">Data Story Maker</h3>
      <p class="cs-exp-desc">Data stories were limited to dashboard screenshots annotated with text — low engagement, hard to produce. I built a prototype that lets an analyst or AI assemble visual narratives using templates, uploaded media, and generated imagery. When developers saw the working demo, several adopted AI tooling into their workflows for the first time.</p>
      <p class="cs-exp-takeaway">The best way to change how a team works is to show them something that already works.</p>

      <h3 class="cs-exp-title">Acquisition Simulator</h3>
      <p class="cs-exp-desc">Evaluating a business acquisition means stress-testing financials across scenarios that spreadsheets make tedious. I built a tool that accepts P&amp;L inputs and models outcomes across conditions — recession, operational changes, added capacity — producing a 12-month projection formatted for a business plan and loan application.</p>
      <p class="cs-exp-takeaway">The best tools solve the problem you have right now.</p>

      <span class="cs-exp-type">Prototype</span>
      <h3 class="cs-exp-title">Guided Analysis</h3>
      <p class="cs-exp-desc">Most BI users know the question they're trying to answer — not the metric or visualization to start with. I prototyped a guided flow that accepts a plain-language question and recommends the right metric, dimension, and chart type. An LLM did the interpretation; the UI surfaced the recommendation. Two flows were fully functional; the rest drove roadmap conversations.</p>
      <p class="cs-exp-takeaway">Starting with a question is more intuitive than starting with a metric.</p>

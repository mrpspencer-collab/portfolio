Offline viewing can be a poor experience on some streaming services — usage rules and availability feel confusing and arbitrary. I led the foundational research, journey mapping, and prototyping that defined Disney+'s early offline viewing feature: a capability that ultimately launched with unlimited downloads across up to 10 devices and no annual constraints.

## The Challenge

<div class="cs-split">
  <div class="cs-split-body">
    <p>Users with paid subscriptions expected seamless access whether streaming or playing local files. The team recognized offline viewing as a competitive opportunity — but the UX territory was largely unexplored. We needed to understand user behavior, identify pain points, and prototype a foundation before product design began in earnest.</p>

## My Role

I conducted foundational research capturing diverse user experiences — identifying opportunities and pain points across download initiation, device storage management, and content playback. I then partnered with another designer on wireframes and user flows for downloading and storage scenarios, ultimately building a prototype and demo script for executive presentation.

## Research Approach

<div class="cs-columns">
  <div>
    <p class="cs-col-label">Round 1 — Discovery</p>
    <ul>
      <li>12 semi-structured interviews with streaming service users</li>
      <li>Focused on recall of past offline download experiences</li>
      <li>Goal: identify mental models, pain points, and expectations</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div>
    <p class="cs-col-label">Round 2 — Validation</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Feedback evaluation on wireframe prototypes</li>
      <li>Task flows for downloading and deleting content</li>
      <li>Goal: evaluate effectiveness of animations, copy, and iconography</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## Journey Map

<div class="cs-split">
  <div class="cs-split-body">
    <p>Device storage emerged as the critical, underexplored dimension. Users often overlooked capacity until they hit limits — at which point deleting large video files became a significant pain point with no clear path forward.</p>
    <p>Key use cases shaped the journey: city commuters, parents downloading content for car trips, and air travelers planning ahead. Each had distinct storage behaviors and failure modes.</p>

## Final Design

Research and prototyping were transferred to Disney+ product designers months later, validating the foundational work's quality and direction.

<div class="cs-featured">
  <img src="/images/final-olv-design-scaled.jpg" alt="Disney+ Offline Viewing — Final Product Design" />
  <div class="cs-featured-body">
    <ul>
      <li>Unlimited downloads — no annual constraints</li>
      <li>Up to 10 mobile and tablet devices supported</li>
      <li>Clear system status and storage visualization</li>
      <li>Refined iconography and micro-copy</li>
      <li>Industry-leading approach at launch</li>
      <li>Up-front research compressed the downstream design process</li>
    </ul>
